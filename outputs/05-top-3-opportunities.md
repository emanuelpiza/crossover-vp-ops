# Top 3 Strategic Opportunities

**Baseline:** $7,839,131 in trailing-12-month vendor spend across 386 vendors. The three opportunities below represent **$790K – $1,182K in annual savings** (10.1%–15.1% of the base) and concentrate in three areas that one owner can each drive.

All savings ranges are expressed with a low–high band because each initiative carries dependencies (contract end dates, parent-company overlap, scope confirmations) that the dataset does not resolve. The mechanism behind each number is shown so the CFO can challenge the math.

---

## Opportunity 1 — Salesforce Stack Right-Sizing

**Annual savings: $531K – $843K**

- **Spend touched:** Salesforce UK ($3,117,226) + HubSpot Ireland ($32,187) + Kimble Applications ($52,825) = **$3,202,238 (40.9% of total vendor spend)**.
- **Mechanism:**
  - Salesforce post-acquisition seat audit → recover duplicate licenses between acquired company and parent. Industry benchmark = 15–25% of seat spend on the $3.12M base = **$467K – $779K/yr**.
  - HubSpot decommission ($32K/yr) — a second CRM running in parallel is almost always a legacy marketing-ops artifact. Remove after a 30-day usage audit.
  - Kimble Applications ($32K/yr, ~60% recovery) — Salesforce-native PSA; absorb into Salesforce functionality or the parent's PSA.
- **Key dependencies:** Salesforce contract end date and auto-renewal clause; confirmation that Salesforce is not considered strategic-protected by the CEO; parent's existing Salesforce enterprise agreement for negotiation leverage; HubSpot usage audit.
- **Owner:** CFO + CRO, with CEO kept informed.
- **Risk:** cutting seats without a usage audit breaks active pipelines. Phased execution only; no change before the next Salesforce renewal window.

## Opportunity 2 — Office Footprint Consolidation

**Annual savings: $170K – $226K**

- **Spend touched:** $1,132,835 across 11 real-estate vendors in 6 cities.

  | Vendor | Spend | City |
  |---|---:|---|
  | TOG UK Properties | $263,821 | London |
  | Zagrebtower D.O.O. | $183,754 | Zagreb |
  | Innovent Spaces | $147,348 | Chennai |
  | Weking D.O.O. | $144,093 | Zagreb (scope unclear — flagged for investigation) |
  | GPT Space & Co | $133,507 | London (second location) |
  | WeWork Singapore | $64,373 | Singapore |
  | Jones Lang Lasalle NSW | $16,274 | Sydney (broker) |
  | Work Easy Space Solutions | $14,913 | Chennai (second location) |
  | CBRE Limited | $8,395 | UK broker |
  | Common Desk LLC | $3,842 | US coworking |
  | Safestore Ltd | $287 | UK storage |

- **Mechanism:** consolidate duplicate locations city-by-city.
  - **London:** TOG $264K + GPT $134K → keep one, sublease the other. 50% reduction on the smaller = **~$67K/yr**.
  - **Zagreb:** Zagrebtower $184K + Weking $144K → 25% reduction on the combined = **~$82K/yr** (partly contingent on clarifying what Weking actually does).
  - **Chennai:** Innovent $147K + Work Easy $15K → collapse into one location = **~$24K/yr**.
- **Key dependencies:** parent acquirer's office footprint per city; lease end dates and break clauses (not in dataset); Weking D.O.O. scope confirmation; headcount per site.
- **Owner:** COO + CFO, with Head of People.
- **Risk:** early lease exit triggers penalty fees not visible in the data. Forced colocation damages retention. No lease action before a 60-day audit.

## Opportunity 3 — Professional Services Consolidation

**Annual savings: $89K – $113K**

- **Spend touched:** $635,512 in run-rate professional services across 33 vendors.

  | Sub-category | Vendors | Run-rate spend |
  |---|---:|---:|
  | Audit / Tax / Accounting | 14 | $473,065 |
  | Legal firms + notaries | 19 | $117,465 |
  | Boutique advisory (non-deal) | ~5 | ~$45,000 |

- **Excluded:** M&A transaction advisors ($313K — RSM UK Corporate Finance, 4i Advisory, Houlihan Lokey, Vector Capital, SS&C Intralinks, Westbrook Advisers). These are one-time deal fees, not recurring spend.

- **Mechanism:**
  - Anchor audit under BDO (the existing incumbent at $343K) or shift to the parent's Big-4. Terminate redundant mid-tier firms (Grant Thornton, Crowe Horwath, McBurneys, Collards, Eurofast, Taxstudio, Granttree, 4i Management Consulting, Bijeli Pijesak) → ~**$26K/yr** on the non-anchor base. Renegotiate BDO scope at 5% → ~**$17K/yr**.
  - Legal panel: reduce 19 firms to 3–4 regional panel firms. 15% reduction → ~**$18K/yr**.
  - Boutique advisory: renegotiate or terminate ambiguous retainers (Harmonic Group $65K is the largest, currently flagged for investigation). Range: **$28K–$52K/yr**.
- **Key dependencies:** confirm BDO is not the parent's incumbent; Harmonic Group scope clarification; mid-integration audit stability.
- **Owner:** CFO (audit/tax) + General Counsel (legal).
- **Risk:** switching the statutory auditor during Year 1 post-close creates regulatory risk and re-audit costs that wipe out two years of savings. Default action is renegotiate, not switch.
