# MEMO — Vendor Spend Review (Year 1 Post-Acquisition)

**To:** CEO, CFO **From:** VP of Operations
**Re:** Rationalizing the $7.84M vendor stack — **$790K–$1.18M in annual savings (10–15%)**.

## Findings and savings

Trailing-12-month spend = **$7,839,131 across 386 vendors**. Top 20 vendors = 75% of spend; Salesforce alone = 40%. Savings concentrate in three initiatives:

**1. Salesforce stack right-sizing — $531K–$843K / yr.** Covers Salesforce ($3.12M) + HubSpot ($32K, a redundant second CRM running in parallel) + Kimble PSA ($53K). Post-acquisition seat audits on Salesforce typically recover 15–25% of license spend via duplicate-seat consolidation with the parent company plus renewal renegotiation ($467K–$779K). Decommission HubSpot as a duplicate ($32K). Absorb Kimble PSA into Salesforce-native or the parent's PSA ($32K). Owner: CFO + CRO.

**2. Office footprint consolidation — $170K–$226K / yr.** 11 real-estate vendors, $1.13M across 6 cities. London has two coworking vendors (TOG $264K + GPT $134K → keep one, ~$67K). Zagreb has two facilities vendors (Zagrebtower $184K + Weking $144K → ~$82K). Chennai has two coworking setups (Innovent $147K + Work Easy $15K → ~$24K). Owner: COO + CFO.

**3. Professional services consolidation — $89K–$113K / yr.** 33 audit, tax, and legal vendors totaling $635K recurring. Anchor audit under BDO (or the parent's Big-4 incumbent) and terminate duplicative mid-tier firms ($26K + $17K BDO scope renegotiation). Reduce 19 legal firms to a panel of 3–4 regional firms ($18K). Renegotiate or terminate opaque advisory retainers like Harmonic Group at $65K ($28K–$52K). Owner: CFO + General Counsel.

## Implementation process

Each initiative has one accountable owner and a clear first action. Start by pulling the contract data the dataset doesn't contain — renewal dates, auto-renewal clauses, break clauses — and freezing renewals above $10K pending review. Run the Salesforce seat audit and the lease/headcount audit in parallel to compress elapsed time. Decommission redundant software (HubSpot) early since it has the cleanest scope. Renegotiate anchor contracts (Salesforce, BDO) at their existing renewal windows to avoid mid-term break costs. Execute lease exits phased with team relocations, not in one step.

## Timeline

- **Week 1:** authorize both audits; issue contract-data requests; freeze renewals >$10K.
- **Weeks 2–5:** run Salesforce seat audit with the CRO; run real-estate break-clause + headcount audit with the COO; open investigation on the two largest opaque vendors (Cloudcrossing $209K, Weking $144K).
- **Weeks 6–9:** decommission HubSpot; plan Kimble migration; RFP for non-anchor professional-services firms; start Salesforce renegotiation.
- **Months 3–6:** execute contracted changes as renewal windows hit; phase lease exits alongside team relocations.
- **Month 6:** validate realized savings against baseline and re-plan Year 2.

## Risks

- **Contract visibility gap.** The dataset has spend but not contract end dates or auto-renewal clauses. If Salesforce auto-renewed recently, we're locked for another cycle and ~$600K of Opportunity 1 slips to Year 2.
- **Lease exit fees not in the data.** Early lease termination penalties could materially reduce Opportunity 2's net savings in Year 1 — the 60-day audit is there to size this risk before committing.
- **Auditor switching risk.** Changing the statutory auditor during Year 1 post-close creates regulatory + re-audit risk that wipes out two years of savings. Default action is renegotiate scope within BDO, not switch.
- **Opaque-vendor exposure.** A further $844K sits with vendors whose scope is unclear from the data — Cloudcrossing ($209K), Weking ($144K), Harmonic Group ($65K), HR Solution International ($88K combined). Realistically 25–35% of this is recoverable after scope mapping, returning with a commit-or-drop decision by Month 3.

**Bottom line:** $790K–$1.18M in realistic annual savings across three initiatives with one owner each. Worst case (dependencies fail): ~$150K floor. Best case: ~$1.18M. Salesforce audit + real-estate audit need a green light this week.
